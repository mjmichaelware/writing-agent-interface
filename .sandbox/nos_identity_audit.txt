﻿CHAPTER 10: FORSAKEN

The interior of the goat-hair sanctuary carried the particular weight of a room where words have been spoken at length and received in silence.

For a daughter of the Peleset—broad-boned, deep-voiced, the kind of woman whose craft pressed down into the dense, layered record of the valley floor rather than reaching upward at the cold and assessless dark above it—this goat-hair tent, sewn tight against the Jezreel wind, was not shelter. It was second skin. A dark womb built to keep the frantic, staccato percussion of the self from the cold, judgmental eye of the deep sky. A space where she could pretend that Ba'al-Zebub of Ekron still leaned in from the low places, still weighed the petition, still kept the long account she had been depositing into for thirty years—even as the silence in the tent's corners settled the way moth settles into neglected gold: consuming one patient layer at a time, until what had been solid certainty was mostly void. I should know. I was sitting in the corner when it happened.
