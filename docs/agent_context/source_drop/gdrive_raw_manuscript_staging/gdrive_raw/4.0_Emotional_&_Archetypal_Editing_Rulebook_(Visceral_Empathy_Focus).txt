I. Visceral Empathy & Relatability Rules
* Replace Detachment with Somatic Cost: When Dan makes a great spiritual decision (e.g., leaving Bethel, refusing silver), the text MUST emphasize the physical pain and emotional cost of that choice.
 * Instead of: "Dan felt a cold resolve."

 * Fear: Expressed as a clenched gut, heartbeat a trapped bird, or cold sweat that stings the eyes.
 * Temptation: Expressed through cloying scents (perfume, spiced meat), hypnotic warmth, or a deep, animal hunger that overrides reason (e.g., the Edomite woman's allure, the comfort of Bethel).
 * Asceticism/Fasting: (CRITICAL for Chapter 10) Describe the stabbing hunger, the dry rasp of the throat, the dizziness of depleted glucose, and the mind's resulting ecstatic clarity as the body submits. This is the ultimate Living Sacrifice.

* Kasha's Perspective (Chapter 11): Her chapter should anchor the emotional landscape, showing her humanity (grief, anger, loss, love) as a profound counterpoint to Dan's extreme, almost inhuman Ascent.
