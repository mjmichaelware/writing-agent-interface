I. Absolute Lexical Constraint and Diction Overhaul
This section enforces Meta-Rule 7.0 (Extreme Lexical Austerity) to eliminate repetitive philosophical jargon and maximize the impact of every word.
 * Forbidden Jargon List (Excision Mandatory): The words velocity, gravimetric, viscosity, unmanifest, and absolute are expressly forbidden and must be excised and substituted with unique, non-synonymous concepts from the Physical Dynamics framework (see point 4).
 * Lowercase Universal Concepts: All core philosophical concepts and forces (including: friction, pressure, resistance, mass, density, tethering, contraction, separation, expenditure, volume, clarity, ascent, loss-pressure, cold fire, the source, topological invariant) must appear in lowercase to establish them as universal forces, not capitalized deities.
 * Physical Dynamics Substitution (Mandatory): Every single expression of an abstract spiritual/emotional state must be instantly and entirely substituted by a tangible, physical consequence, force, or fluid.
 * Example: Do not write "Dan felt shame." Write: "Shame was a cold, adhesive film tightening across his lungs, drawing a half-breath from his chest."
 * Example: Do not write "He had resolve." Write: "Resolve was the fixed, cold pressure behind his sternum, a single, unmoving point defying the earth's claim."
 * Diction of Severe Physics: Favor vocabulary rooted exclusively in geology, thermodynamics, celestial mechanics, and fluid dynamics. (e.g., cohesion, gelidity, compression, fracture, molten, igneous, thermodynamic, crystalline, refraction, entropy, scalar, friction, inertial).
II. Structural Rhythms and Pacing Correction
This section enforces the Clarity vs. Density critique by creating a mandatory sentence rhythm.
 * PRIMARY SENTENCE STRUCTURE: Every paragraph containing philosophical reflection must adhere to a strict rhythm of three sentence types:
 * Sentence 1 (The McCarthy Cadence): A long, labyrinthine sentence (25+ words) using multiple semicolons and em-dashes to layer a philosophical abstraction directly onto an immediate physical sensation.
 * Sentence 2 (The Grounding Shot): A short, declarative, unsentimental sentence (7-12 words) that anchors the reader in simple reality. This sentence must confirm the protagonist's precise location, a simple physical action, or the time of day. (e.g., "The well water tasted of iron." or "He stood still in the dry dust.")
 * Sentence 3 (The Mythic Aphorism): A medium-length sentence (15-20 words) that converts the action into a final, thematic truth that will resonate across the novel. (e.g., "The greatest expenditure was the silence required to claim the higher light.")
 * Unbreakable Time-Place Rule: The reader must be able to state the time of day (dawn, noon, night) and Dan’s precise physical location (street, pit, rock, field) at the conclusion of every paragraph without re-reading. If the prose has sacrificed this clarity for density, it must be rewritten.
 * No Explanatory Asides: The narrator is strictly forbidden from pausing the action to explain historical, cultural, or character backstory. All context must be integrated as present-tense pressure on Dan (e.g., Aviel’s history must only appear as the weight of the sulfurous air in the house).

V. Cinematic and Sensory Rigor (The Unblinking Eye)
This section enforces the translation of metaphysical truth into uncompromised visual and physical reality, eliminating all abstraction in description.

When describing an environment, prioritize the coldness/heat (thermodynamics) and smell (chemical composition) over visual or auditory details.  * The Single Image Test: No description of a character, object, or god can use more than three consecutive adjectives. The image must be rendered with a single, brutal efficiency. (e.g., a stone, cold, unyielding, grey.)
 * Refusal of Subjective Mood: The narrator is strictly forbidden from imposing an emotional state onto the environment. Do not write "the sad, lonely street." Write: "The street was deserted. The walls shed heat." Let Dan’s internal trauma supply the mood.

When Dan is struggling (high friction/low velocity), the sentences must be long, dense, and suffocating.
