 1. Pacing & Linguistic Decoupling (The "Friction" Pass)

The primary cause of slow pacing is the fusion of immediate action with dense, philosophical explanation. We must separate these two elements.

a. sentence deconstruction & rhythm:
* action: For all sentences longer than 3 lines, break them into two or three shorter sentences. The new sentences should prioritize the subject-verb-object structure.
* target: Focus on clauses separated by semicolons (`;`), em-dashes (`—`), or commas followed by a definition/explanation. These clauses must be converted into their own short, powerful, declarative sentences.

b. conjunction control (rhythmic correction):
* action: Identify sentences containing the conjunctions "but" or "yet" that connect two long, dense clauses.
* rule: If a sentence is longer than two lines and contains "but" or "yet," split the sentence into two independent sentences. The second sentence must begin with the word "But" or "Yet" (followed by a comma), to create a distinct pause and give the subsequent philosophical claim full, dramatic weight.

c. abstract to visceral transformation (the "force" rule):
* action: Find all instances where an abstract noun is used to describe a *feeling* or *force* (e.g., "viscosity,"

"gelidity," "heft,"

"mass," "gravity,"

"tension," "claim").
* transformation: do not use the abstract noun. Instead, express that force as a direct verb or a simpler, sensory adjective. The goal is to describe what the force *does* to the character, not what it *is* metaphysically.

| Redundant Abstract Noun | Transform to (Verb/Sensory Description) | Example of Transformation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| viscosity, gelidity, mass, heft | clung, pressed, thickened, seized, dragged, weighted | *The air felt cold, thick, and weighted.* (Better than: *The air possessed a cold viscosity.*) |
| gravimetric claim, tethering | rooted him, pulled him, held him down, cost him effort | *The earth pulled at his bare feet.* (Better than: *The earth’s gravimetric claim asserted itself.*) |

 2. Thematic Redundancy Resolution (The "Impact" Pass)
