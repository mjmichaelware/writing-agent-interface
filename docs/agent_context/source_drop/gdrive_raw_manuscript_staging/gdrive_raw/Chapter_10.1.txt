Chapter 10: The Ascent 

The air, already thin and sharp at the base of Hermon, bit at Daniel’s lungs with the first step. The ground, still damp from Rafa’s cleansing tears, felt cold beneath his bare feet, a stark contrast to the warmth of the giant's parting words. He looked up, his single eye taking in the colossal, snow-capped sentinel that pierced the sky like a spear. Its upper reaches were shrouded in a permanent, icy shroud, even in the warmth of the season below, a stark reminder of the heights he sought. Where the veil is thinnest, his father had whispered. The words echoed, a new mantra, silencing the lingering ache of separation from Rafa, the last true kindness he had known. He gripped the smooth, heavy dagger Rafa had given him, its inner light a faint, reassuring hum against his palm, a tangible connection to the ancient power that had once sought to escape the Flood. His walking stick, worn smooth by countless miles, became an extension of his will, his only other possession. He had nothing else. No silver, no sandals, no water container. Only the clothes on his back, the stick, the dagger, and the unyielding purpose in his heart. He was a man stripped bare, like Job, with nothing left to lose but the very essence of his striving.
The path was steep, winding like a serpent coiling around the mountain’s flank. Each step was a deliberate act of will, a push against the unseen forces that seemed to cling to the very rock. The air grew colder, thinner, stealing the warmth from his bones. The biting chill of the raw stone seeped into his exposed soles, a relentless ache that climbed his shins. He had not gone far when the sky, which had been a clear, indifferent blue, began to bruise. Dark, churning clouds, the color of old lead and bruised fruit, boiled up from the horizon, swallowing the sun whole. A low, guttural growl rumbled in the distance, growing swiftly into a deafening roar. The very air felt like a clenched fist, tightening around him, a heavy, suffocating pressure that seemed to squeeze the breath from his lungs.
Then, the sky ripped open. Not with rain, but with a torrent of raw, unbridled fury. The first drops struck him like flung pebbles, cold and sharp, quickly escalating into a torrential downpour that seemed to seek to drown him where he stood. The wind, a furious, invisible hand, tore at his cloak, whipping his messy black hair across his face, blinding his single eye. Lightning, jagged as broken bones, ripped across the sky, momentarily illuminating the mountain in blinding flashes, each one followed by a thunderclap that vibrated through his very teeth, rattling his skull, a primal scream of opposition. This was the raw, untamed voice of Lord Baal, the Storm God, roaring his displeasure at Daniel's upward aim, seeking to cast him down with the might of a tempest.
A suffocating heat, thick and cloying, began to rise from the very stones, clinging to his skin, making each breath a struggle. It was the oppressive weight of a grave, pressing down on him, stealing his strength, making his limbs feel like lead. A profound lethargy seeped into his bones, making every movement an act of monumental effort. He stumbled, his walking stick slipping on the slick rock, and a wave of nausea washed over him, leaving him clammy and weak. His skin prickled with a phantom fever, chills running through him despite the oppressive heat, and his throat felt raw, as if he had swallowed dust and ashes. The cold of the raw stone against his bare feet intensified, a merciless bite, a constant reminder of his stripped vulnerability. The earth itself seemed to exhale a sigh of profound weariness, the very breath of Lord Mot, drawing him into its endless, quiet slumber.
Then came the whispers. They slithered from the shadows of the rocks, unseen, unheard by the ear, but felt in the deepest parts of his mind. Turn back, little one. This path is not for you. This strength you cling to… it is a fragile thing. It will break. You are alone. Always alone. What is the point of striving when all will be lost? A sudden, inexplicable fear bloomed in his chest, cold and sharp, urging him to flee, to abandon the climb, to seek the safety of the lowlands, where other humans huddled for warmth. His Courage, a tight knot in his gut, felt as though it were unraveling, thread by agonizing thread, leaving him exposed and trembling. The very air seemed to thin around his resolve, leaving him vulnerable, a warrior without armor.
The ground beneath his bare soles became treacherous. It shifted beneath his weight, sometimes giving a strange, soft give, like stepping onto thick moss, but then turning slick and unstable, as if the very rock had dissolved into a watery mire. A disorienting mist, thick and cold, swirled up from unseen crevices, clinging to his face, making him feel as though he were drowning in air, each breath a struggle against the suffocating dampness. The path ahead seemed to twist and contort, impossibly complex, a labyrinth designed to confuse and deter. His mind, usually sharp and clear, felt clouded, his thoughts scattering like dust in the wind. This path… it makes no sense. It is ill-designed. You cannot navigate this. You are a creature of the earth, like all others, bound to its currents. The mountain itself seemed to writhe beneath him, a living, chaotic entity seeking to throw him from its back, as if the very essence of Lord Yam, the Prince of the Sea, churned the rock to mire.
Through the blurring haze of rain and fatigue, he saw them. Shimmering, ethereal figures, beautiful beyond mortal comprehension, their forms sculpted from moonlight and ripe fields, appeared at the edges of his vision. They danced, their movements fluid and inviting, beckoning him towards soft beds and warm hearths, whispering promises of companionship, of gentle hands and soft breaths. A profound, aching Loneliness bloomed in his chest, a hunger for connection so intense it brought tears to his single eye, mingling with the rain. This, he knew, was the deceptive allure of Lord Astarte, seeking to distract him with the siren call of companionship, twisting his natural need for connection into a torment of absence. Memories of his mother, of the family in Dothan, of the simple warmth of shared bread, flashed before him, twisting into a cruel reminder of the solace he had abandoned. The very air around him seemed to whisper of missed embraces, of empty spaces in his heart.
His stomach, a hollow drum, began to gnaw at itself, a sharp, insistent Hunger that intensified with every step. He saw visions of succulent roasted lamb, of fresh bread steaming from the oven, of ripe, bursting figs. The physical craving was overwhelming, a desperate, primal urge to simply stop, to gorge himself, to fill the vast emptiness within, to be rooted to the earth by the simple, undeniable needs of the flesh. The very scent of the barren rock seemed to mock his empty belly, a constant reminder of what the earth withheld.
Then came the fire, not from the sky, but from within, a searing heat that consumed, not stifled. It crawled up his legs, an invisible blaze that scorched his skin, leaving phantom blisters in its wake. He felt a deep, piercing throb in the scar of his missing eye, and a dull, constant ache in his joints, old wounds flaring with new, inexplicable pain. A sense of malevolent eyes, unseen, watchful, seemed to press from the dark recesses of the rocks, judging his every weakness, his every faltering breath. His very flesh seemed to turn against him, a betrayal from within, as if Lord Resheph, the god of plague, sought to consume him from the inside out.
Each step was a battle, not just against the mountain, but against unseen hands that sought to pull him down, to break his spirit, to root him firmly in the earth. His strength faltered, his legs trembling, his will a flickering candle flame in a gale. Doubt, cold and sharp, pricked at the edges of his resolve. Why this suffering? Why this endless striving for a height that offers only cold indifference? Turn back. Find comfort, find ease, find warmth. And from the very rock beneath his bare feet, a deep, resonant hum, a pervasive, crushing weight, seemed to rise, trying to pin him, to absorb him into the ancient, unyielding stone. It was the vast, silent authority of the mountain itself, the very power of Lord El, the High God of the Canaanites, demanding he submit, that he cease his upward striving, that he remain bound to their dominion. Above it all, the sun, when it broke through the clouds, offered no solace, only a cold, indifferent glare, a vast, unfeeling eye that seemed to judge his every faltering step, a reminder of the unchanging, unyielding order of the cosmos. He was being tested, like Job, stripped of everything, even the capacity for negative states, burned away by the fires of his trials. The principle of those who have nothing, from whom even more will be taken, was laid bare in his torment, a painful shedding of old flesh, a rebirth in fire.
But then, a pinpoint of light, impossibly pure and unwavering, pierced through the churning darkness above him. It was not the sun, nor a star, but something else, something that called to the deepest part of him, a silent, insistent beckoning. Up, his spirit cried, echoing his mother’s voice from a time long past. Always up. The dagger in his hand felt warm, a tiny ember of defiance against the overwhelming cold. He gritted his teeth, digging his bare toes into the treacherous rock, finding purchase where there was none, refusing to yield. His single eye, though blurred by rain and tears, fixed on that distant, unwavering light. He was a small, defiant figure against the colossal wrath of the sky, a fragile vessel battling a cosmic storm. And yet, he held. He was shedding skin, burning away the old, the weak, the fearful, just as the Phoenix rises from ash. Each torment was a cleansing fire, each pain a lesson in the necessity of release.
The ascent became a blur of agony and stubborn refusal. The cold bit into his bare feet, the phantom fevers burned, the loneliness gnawed, the hunger clawed. He stumbled, fell, scraped his knees on the unforgiving rock, but always, always, he pushed himself up. He was a creature of pure, unyielding will, a single thread of defiance against the vast tapestry of opposition. The mountain seemed to stretch endlessly, its summit a cruel, distant promise. He felt a palpable pressure in the air, a silent roar against his very presence, against his daring to aim higher than their domain. He would not break. He would not yield. He was a cup, now empty of all earthly desire, ready to be filled with something boundless, a foundation built not on possessions, but on pure, upward intention. His very existence was becoming a testament to the upward aim, a living prayer against the downward pull.
Finally, after what felt like an eternity, the slope began to ease. The wind, though still biting, lost its furious edge. The rain lessened, becoming a fine, cold mist. The oppressive heat lifted, replaced by the crisp, clean air of the heights. He pushed through the last few meters, his legs screaming, his lungs burning, and then, he stood.
He stood at the very top of Mount Hermon. The air was thin, almost painfully so, but exhilarating. The ground beneath his feet, now bare rock, was cold, but the chill no longer bit. Snow, ancient and undisturbed, lay in patches in sheltered crevices, a testament to the mountain's enduring height, even in the warmth of the season below. It was feasible, barely, for a human to reach this summit, but only one driven by an unearthly will.
The sky above was no longer a tumultuous storm, but a vast, inky canvas, studded with a million indifferent stars, their light sharp and cold. The air was thin, pure, and utterly silent, save for the faint whisper of the wind. He looked around, and saw them. Not monuments, not altars, but two hundred holes in the ground, perfectly circular, as if something immense had fallen from the heavens and punched through the very rock. They were ancient, scarred, their edges worn smooth by millennia, like the mouths of forgotten wells leading to unimaginable depths. These were the marks of things fallen long ago, beings that had chosen a downward path, leaving these silent, gaping wounds in the earth. The air around them felt heavy, a lingering echo of immense power and profound regret, a testament to the duality of existence, the necessary darkness that defines the light. For even the Source of All, in its infinite goodness, had cast away parts of itself, a painful but necessary shedding, creating the very contrasts that allowed for ascent.
Daniel, his body utterly spent, the giant’s dagger still clutched in his hand, its inner light now a faint, almost imperceptible pulse, stumbled forward. The weight of the world, the battle with the unseen, the sheer, unrelenting effort of the climb, finally claimed him. He fell face down onto the cold, hard rock of the summit, the dagger slipping from his grasp to lie beside him. His breath hitched, then steadied, then grew shallow. His body, battered and broken, finally yielded to the profound exhaustion.
He was asleep. A sleep so deep, so utterly still, that it felt like the very breath of the universe had paused. An eternal rest. His earthly vessel, having served its purpose, was now ready to be shed, like a snake’s old skin, painful in its leaving, but necessary for the new.
His mind, however, was already soaring. It was the Dreamscape, yes, but unlike any he had known. There was no effort, no struggle. He simply was moving. Up.
He shot past the moon, a colossal pearl now, not cold and indifferent, but smiling, a gentle, knowing presence. He laughed, a soundless, joyful burst that echoed through the void. He surged past the stars, not distant pinpricks, but vast, welcoming suns, their light warm and inviting, their ancient eyes now smiling at him too. He raced past groups of stars, then past groups of groups of stars, and then past those groups of groups of groups – galaxies, clusters of galaxies, superclusters – leaving the very universe behind, a tiny, receding speck of light in the boundless expanse. He was moving beyond the physical, beyond the known, his essence expanding, becoming one with the infinite.
And as he ascended, something shifted within him. The emptiness left by the pit, the stripping away of his capacity for negative states (cast away out of fear, like a painful shedding of old flesh), had made him a vessel. Now, that vessel was filling, not with anything tangible, but with realms of being, each more real, more vibrant than the last, leading him further up and further in.
He rose. Where the sticky miasma of Lust had promised false warmth and delivered emptiness, a crystalline Purity now permeated his very essence. A profound Fulfillment sang through his being, needing no outward object, no physical touch, only the quiet knowing of perfect completeness. His essence swelled into currents of Heavenly Abundance, not something possessed, but something he was, a boundless fount for all. This realm shimmered with a soft, inner luminescence, like moonlight filtered through clean water, its air tasting of dew and nascent life, the very breath of a reality more solid and joyous than any he had known.
Higher still. The grasping void of Gluttony dissolved, and the scorching heat of Anger faded into cool air. An absolute Serenity settled deep within his core, a Cooling Presence washing over him like balm, leaving behind only the most gentle calm, a profound Healing Essence that resonated through him. Then, the thorny thicket of Resentment gave way to universal Forgiveness, unfurling a seamless Connection to all things that bound him in a silken embrace. His spirit flowed with a Smoothness that knew no friction, no sharp edge. This plane was a tapestry of interwoven light, where every thread, every being, pulsed with an undeniable, gentle harmony, leading him deeper into the heart of things.
He passed the ragged edges of Despair and the heavy sorrow, those buried stones of Sadness. Luminous Hope pulsed within him, an unyielding current. His will, once fractured, was utterly Resilient, a core of Wholeness that sang with an undiminished vibrance, unbreakable and eternal. An effervescent Joy now danced through him, making him weightless. His spirit soared freely, Unburdened, a laughter echoing through the silent expanse. This realm hummed with a quiet, vibrant energy, like a thousand tiny bells chiming in perfect unison, each sound a testament to endless possibility, a boundless, sun-drenched meadow of pure light where unseen currents of delight lifted him higher and higher, always further in.
The bitter cloud of Envy dissolved, and the gaping voids left by failed Courage filled with a true, unwavering light. Pure Admiration for the beauty of all creation filled him, a deep Contentment resonating through his boundless spirit, a Shared Joy expanding his presence infinitely. Unwavering Courage pulsed within him, an infinite wellspring of Boundless Strength, a fearless embrace of the unknown, for fear had no place in this truer world. This realm was a symphony of light and color, where every hue and every form sang a silent song of interconnected wonder, a vast, open sky where winds of pure resolve propelled him onward, and distant, towering peaks of light beckoned, drawing him ever more deeply into the heart of reality.
The insatiable desire of Entitlement and the devouring nature of Pride simply receded like shadows before the dawn. Profound Humility embraced him like a soft cloak, a boundless Gratitude opening his spirit wide. He became a conduit for Giving, an unending flow outwards, and a vessel for Receiving without demand. Ultimate Love unfolded, a complete Self-effacement becoming his state, a profound Unity with all existence, a single, pulsating heart of compassion, intimately connected to every star, every whisper, every breath. This highest plane was a boundless ocean of pure, undifferentiated light, where all distinctions dissolved into a single, radiant consciousness, the very beginning of the Great Story.
The yearning of the things below him, the cries for help, the silent prayers – they began to resonate within him, not as external sounds, but as the very voice of his own mind. All he heard now were the myriad voices of others, cleaning out the heavens, a symphony of longing. He felt an immense, overwhelming love bloom in his heart, a love for everything beneath him, even the dead courage and tainted hope, the gnawing envy and burning anger, the bitter resentment that had once clung to him. He understood them now, having met them, having been born of flesh and subject to their pull. He understood the burdens, the failures, the desperate clinging to the earth. Not because he knew every intricate story, for human hearts were too complex for full comprehension – indeed, who could ever truly know another's story, or even their own, in all its tangled complexity? – but because he knew the essence of their struggle, having walked through his own hell. And in that understanding, in that boundless, all-encompassing love, he became.
He became a deity of love.
And so, the boy from Hebron, whose life had been measured in burdens and entanglements, whose journey had been a relentless climb against the very forces of the earth, soared beyond the stars. He was no longer a seeker, but the sought. He was no longer a judge, but the embodiment of understanding. His physical form, from the cold, hard summit of Hermon, was simply taken, vanishing without a trace, just as some ancient texts whispered of those who had ascended before him. No one ever saw him again. And the dagger, left behind on the cold, hard summit of Hermon, gleamed faintly in the starlight, a silent testament to a journey that had ended not in rest, but in a profound, eternal becoming, a love so vast it encompassed every broken thing below. For Daniel, this was not an ending, but the very beginning of the real story, a journey further up and further in than any mortal mind could comprehend, into the vast, radiant heart of all things.
