 I. THE CORE METAPHYSICAL LAWS

 The "I AM" vs. The "Gods of Things": The Vertical Source is pure motion/being. The Canaanite gods are "Horizontal" fragments of the material world (lust, greed, dust).
 The Fragmented Self: The gods (Dagon, Beelzebub, Astarte) are Dan’s own psychological drives looking back at him from the "inside out." Encountering a god is an act of internal mirror-imaging.
 The Quantum Dust: The Dreamscape (Jungian Collective Subconscious) is made of "Dust"—Planck-length cells containing the potential for a Big Bang in every unit. Without Will and Intent (Spirit), these particles collapse into nothingness.
 The Sacrifice of Judgment: To move from Dan (He who judges the fragments) to Daniel (God is my Judge), the protagonist must undergo a "thermodynamic zero-point" descent in the Pit at Megiddo, losing his voice (human language) to gain crystalline vertical clarity.

 II. KEY ARCHETYPES & ENTITIES

 The Trinity: Established in Ch. 1: Father (Aviel/Entropy), Mother (Zuna/Vision), Son (Dan/Volition).
 Father Aviel: A scholar collapsed into "systemic decay." His hoarding is a literal sacrifice to Dagon, the god of accumulation. His house is a monument to "Potential Rigorously Refused."
 Beelzebub (The Deceptive Light): A head taller than men, impossibly handsome, bored by his own victory. He has a nervous tick: rubbing his head (The Fall) and checking a watch frozen at 6:00 (The invention of Time). He created Isabel (via Kasha) to have something to control.
 Kasha (The Witch of Jezreel): Not evil, but ignorant of the "Vertical." Her 7-day Dreamscape journey (6 days of Princes/Sins, 7th day of Nothing) mirrors a corrupted creation.
 The Cat Narrator: A "silent watcher" with a tail "longer than a thread," who refuses to narrate the "coitus" of Isabel’s creation due to its inherent horror.
 Rafa: The Giant of the heights; represents the "last true kindness" and provides the Sword of Will.

 III. NARRATIVE BEATS & SYMBOLISM

 The Hebron House: A four-room map of a divided heart. A "structural emptiness" where the withdrawal of Aviel’s will creates the vacuum for Dan’s to be born.
 The Philistine Iron: Knowledge unearned (The Tower of Babel/Promethean Fire). The Philistines defeat Israel easily because they use technology to bypass spiritual growth, which bores Beelzebub.
 The Mother’s Homage: Mother Zuna is "Nameless here forevermore" (The Raven). The Hebrew word for "Name" is the currency of the spirit.
 The Ending (Stellar Cannibalism): The cycle of Cain and Abel projected onto the stars. The duller, resentful twin devours the brighter brother. The ultimate unresolved question: "Here we go again."

 IV. SUPREME REVISION CONSTRAINTS

 The Law of Immediacy: Philosophical depth must be felt through sensory detail (e.g., "viscous drag of the road") rather than explained through academic jargon.
 The Paragraph Mandate: Visual density for traditional publishing (4–8 sentences). No choppy, web-style writing.
 Voice Synthesis: Narrator: Warm, editorializing, direct address (Lewis).
 Interjections: Sardonic, witty, warning the reader (Snicket).
